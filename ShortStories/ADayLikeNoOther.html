These friends had been through a lot together, but tension can bubble to the surface quickly with Marco’s harsh tongue sometimes.

"Sure, sure. We all know I'm the most creative out of everyone here. When I get back home, I’m going to be a writer". There was a pause in the conversation. Virgil looked over at Richards and Marco who were both holding their breath for a moment, before all 3 exploded with laughter. He seemed to be chuckling along to the joke at his own expense.

"Noah, I love you buddy, but not even I want to hear you talk" Virgil threw in. That only made Richards, Marco, and Noah laugh even louder.

"All 4 of you, shut your damn mouths! Did you forget we’re on a battlefield!?" hissed Sarge and all 4 soldiers immediately shut it. Sarge was harsh but everyone respected him. He was as by the books as you can get. Orders are orders and need to be carried out regardless. Virgil found him a bit of a buzzkill most of the time, but without him, this group would be off the rails and definitely all dead.

As if Sarge wasn’t enough to kill the mood, another hot gust of wind came by and sapped out even more energy out of the group. Virgil could feel himself sweating out all the water left in his body. He looked up at the sun almost as if to say a silent curse. The same damn sun that has been tormenting them day after day in an endless loop like there's no escape.

A short series of electronic beeps followed by a loud static with bleary voices mixed in blared up from behind Virgil. Sarge was getting another transmission. All 4 soldiers turned around to face Sarge and see what he said. Getting a transmission was nothing new. Plenty of orders have come in from central, re-routing their plan again and again. It was never quite clear what exactly they were accomplishing, but it's not their job to know. Virgil was sometimes overwhelmed with the feeling of being a small cog in a much larger machine. These orders were to serve a specific purpose for some greater whole, and that whole simply can’t be visualized from his vantage point. He's too in the thick of it. Then again, he always thought he wouldn't like what he saw if he ever did get to see.

Sarge was assessing the situation as calmly as he could.

The doors were loose and the windows broken, but the walls were made of concrete and all they needed right now was a wall. Noah sprinted directly to the building without hesitation. Sarge made a motion with his arm, signaling to follow as he darted ahead. Richards was still cowering on the ground. "There is no helicopter coming. There never is"

"What is my mother’s name?!" Noah screamed in anger, as if accusing Sarge of something.

"Noah, what the hell are you talking about? Calm down" Virgil responded in a worried tone. Noah had never acted like this and it was distressing to see the person normally leaned on for levity break down like this.

"Oh my god, what does her face look like? What is my last name? Where am I from?" Virgil looked to Richards to connect his worry for Noah. Richards had looked up from his hands at this but hadn't made eye contact with Virgil yet.

"War can be straining, son," said Sarge. "It can do terrible things to the mind but now is no time to lose it. Help is on its way".

Noah looked up at Sarge and stared right through him in a way that chilled Virgil. "No there isn't," he responded once again. Noah stood up and looked out the view of the window for a moment before turning his attention to the door.

"What the hell are you doing soldier!? Get your ass back down or you’ll get your cranium shot off your fucking shoulders." Noah ignored him and started walking to the door.

"Noah, I don’t know what you think you’re doing right now, but calm down and think this through" Virgil pleaded. Noah brushed past both their screams and walked out the door away from the building.
