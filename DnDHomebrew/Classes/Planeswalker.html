Planeswalker

Deep within a murky swamp, a woman dressed in purple animates the corpses of those who have come for her.

In a flash of light before a radiant sunset, a man dressed in blue and white appears and bends the magic of the air around him to slow time to a crawl.

Diving within the heat of conflict in a bustling city, a rebel draws on the innate magic of the drums and chants of war to lead his pack of giant boars into battle.

Planeswalkers are explorers of the multiverse and their magic draws on their experiences and encounters to channel the 5 colors of mana through their innate planeswalker spark.

Planeswalker Spark

Each planeswalker traces their magic back to their spark, a magical quirk tied to their very soul. Each living creature has an incredibly rare chance to be randomly born with a latent planeswalker spark, and only after a moment of intense emotion (often traumatically) will the spark ignite and the planeswalker will unconsciously jump to a random location in the multiverse. It is there that they will begin to control their spark and eventually (hopefully) make it back home.

Their planeswalker spark allows them to harness the 5 colors of manna, white, blue, black, red, and green, all described later in this class. Planeswalkers call upon their experiences in these alien worlds and channel manna through their spark to recreate what they have witnessed.

Every mile of land, every living (and unliving) creature, every cosmic event, every miniscule personal event, and every spell wielded by other magic uses contains manna, and it is from these events that the planeswalkers weave their spells.

The Multiverse In D&D

The Dungeons and Dragons multiverse contains a number of officially published worlds, like The Forgotten Realms, Dragonlance, and Eberron, all contained within crystal spheres and connected via a vast sea of phlogiston, as described in the Planescape setting. This multiverse has also become connected with those described in Magic The Gathering, such as Theros and Ravnica. Planeswalkers are uniquely equipped to jump between these different worlds with their party to explore and discover unimaginable sights.

Not many games wish to jump from world to world, in which case, the planeswalker can still work well. Most D&D settings contain different planes of existence; The Material Plane, The Feywild, The Elemental Chaos, The 9 Hells, The Far Realm, ect. A planeswalker’s abilities can easily be refurbished to instead focus on the different planes rather than entirely different settings.

It's important to discuss expectations with your DM about where the planeswalkers abilities make sense to access during character creation. Remember, even a cleric can work in a setting with no gods in it. The flavor of D&D can be easily adjusted to suit your game.

Creating A Planeswalker

As you create your planeswalker, there are important questions to consider, since their abilities are often directly tied to their past. A planeswalker’s spark can only be ignited and become active after a moment of intense emotion. These moments can be pure joy, but most commonly they are fueled by trauma. What was this event?

Another important question to consider is where did your character first planeswalk to and how did that color their perception of the world? Remember, they are likely reeling from a traumatic moment without any closure as they try to control these new powers. Are they desperately trying to get back home or is this strange new world a nice reprieve?

Lastly, ask what color of mana best suits your character’s personality. The different colors of mana are described later in this class, but a planeswalker is often drawn to one (or multiple) of the 5 colors due to their personal philosophy, and this can change with the character as they grow! A calculating blue-mage who learns the value of community and hope might begin to incorporate white manna within their spells.

Quick Build

You can make a planeswalker quickly by following these suggestions. First, Wisdom should be your highest ability score, followed by Dexterity or Constitution. Second, choose the folk hero background.

Sparkcasting

Your magical planeswalker spark grants you the ability to harness different colors of mana to cast spells. See the end of this class description for the list of planeswalker spells sorted by color.

Cantrips

At 1st level, you know 3 cantrips from the planeswalker spell list. Each cantrip must have at least 1 color within your color identity. For example, if your color identity is only green, you can still get Infestation, despite it being both green and black, but you could not get Chill Touch since that is only black. You gain additional cantrips at higher levels as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Planeswalker table. Additionally, when you level up, you can switch out 1 cantrip you know for a different cantrip, as long as it still meets color requirements.

Mana

There are 5 colors of mana which can be spent on casting spells. When you complete a long rest, you gain an amount of mana equal to your devotion to a color multiplied by your potency. For example, at 5th level, you have 3 potency, and if you have 2 devotion to green, then you would generate 6 green mana for the day. However, if you instead had 1 devotion to green and 1 devotion to black, then you would generate 3 green mana and 3 black mana for the day. You regain expended mana when you finish a long rest.

In order to cast a spell, you must spend mana equal to the cost listed next to it (spells with their costs are listed at the end of the document). For each colored icon, you must spend a mana of the specified color, but the numbers in gray circles can be spent with any color. For example, Meld Into Stone costs [2][g] and requires 3 mana to cast, 1 of which must be green mana and the other 2 can be any color.

Some spells have a hybrid mana symbol and will allow multiple different colors of mana, such as Enthral, which requires [1][ub]. The [ub] can be paid for with either blue mana or black mana and the [1] can still be paid for with any color of mana.

You can spend extra mana on spells should you choose to. For each additional mana spent, the spell is cast 1 spell level higher, to a maximum of 9th level.

Some abilities will grant temporary manna. You have access to temporary manna until the end of your turn, at which point any unspent mana goes away. If you gain temporary mana on another turn, then it lasts until the end of your next turn.

Planeswalkers generally have less magic they can use in a day than other spellcasters but have more flexibility in what spells they want to cast.





Preparing Spells

You prepare a list of planeswalker spells equal to your Wisdom modifier + your planeswalker level (minimum of one spell). The spell cannot be higher than the maximum spell level as shown on the planeswalker table. You can only cast spells that are within your color identity. If a spell has hybrid mana symbols, it counts as being within your color identity if you have either of the required colors.

For example, if you are 5th level and have 2 devotion to green, you can prepare plant growth ([1][g][g]). You could also prepare Aura of Vitality ([1][wg][wg]) since that can count as either white or green. However, you could not prepare Wall of Sand ([1][wr][wg]) because that can only be prepared if you have devotion to white or devotion to both red and green.

You can change your list of prepared spells when you finish a long rest. Preparing a new list of planeswalker spells requires time attuning with the mana of the lands that you’ve traveled through: at least 1 minute per spell level for each spell on your list.

Spellcasting Ability

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your planeswalker spells. The power of your spells comes from your ability to connect with and tap into the mana of the multiverse and all the different forms it takes. You use your Wisdom whenever a planeswalker refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Wisdom modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a planeswalker spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Alternative Spellcasting Abilities

With your DM’s permission, you can change your spellcasting ability and all mentions of wisdom to either intelligence or charisma. Wisdom is the most common ability for planeswalkers as casting spells requires an intimate connection with the multiverse, but in some cases it may make more sense to use charisma for a fiery red-mage who uses their own emotions to fuel their spells, or intelligence a calculating blue-mage who reads the world like a math problem.

Ritual Casting

You can cast a planeswalker spell as a ritual if it has the ritual tag, you have the spell prepared, and it is within your color identity.

Spellcasting Focus

You can use an arcane focus as a spellcasting focus for your planeswalker spells.

Color Identity

At 1st level, you attune with a specific color of mana, either White, Blue, Black, Red, or Green. You gain 1 devotion to that color. Each one is detailed later in the class document. Your color identity determines what colors of mana you generate each day and therefore what spells you can cast, special abilities that can be applied to your summoned creatures, and added features special to the color.

At 5th, 9th, 13th, and 17th level respectively you can increase your devotion to a color again. It does not need to be the same color from the last time you gained this feature. You can choose to further increase your devotion to a color you already have to be able to cast that color’s spells more consistently and gain more powerful abilities or you can gain devotion to a new color, unlocking a wider range of spells and abilities without specializing as much.

For example, if I have 1 devotion to green and I reach 5th level, I can either choose to have 2 devotion to green or I can have 1 devotion to green and 1 devotion to another color, like black for example.

Some planeswalkers focus entirely on 1 color and become a master of that slice of magic, while others branch out to encompass all 5 colors of mana, gaining access to almost every spell in existence. Most tend to average around 2-3.

Most planeswalkers will, consciously or unconsciously, incorporate the literal color of their spark within their clothes, equipment, or on their body. White is sometimes represented with gold, and black with purple.

Summon Lesser Creature

At 2nd level, your connection with the multiverse allows you to manifest echoes of your previous encounters as creatures on the field that fight for you. As an action, you summon a creature to an unoccupied point within 30 ft. It goes immediately after your turn in initiative. The creature disappears once it is reduced to 0 hit points, dies, or after 1 minute. You can also use an action to dismiss it. It is allied to you and follows your verbal commands (no action required by you). If you don’t issue any, it takes the Dodge action and uses its move to avoid danger.

When you summon the lesser creature, you choose its appearance as well as its creature type from the following list: Beast, Construct, Humanoid (also chose the race), Plant, or Undead. In addition, you may choose if it has a walking speed or a swimming speed. It uses the following stat block, which uses your proficiency bonus (PB) and planeswalker level (LV) in several places.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus and regain all expended uses when you finish a short or long rest.

Modified Creatures

When you reach 3rd level, the color of your mana shapes your summons’ forms and powers. When you summon a lesser creature, you can pick one of the following abilities to grant them. An ability can only be chosen if you have at least 1 devotion to at least one of the 2 listed colors. The mana symbols don’t represent an additional cost. They are just there to show devotion requirements.

At 15th level, when you summon a creature, you may spend 3 additional mana to grant that creature another of the following abilities. All of the mana must be of the required colors of that ability, such as spending [w][w][w] to grant the creature double strike or [u][u][g] to grant it ward. Giving a creature an ability in this way ignores the devotion prerequisite.

Flying [wu]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either white or blue

This creature gains a flying speed equal to its fastest speed plus 10 ft.

Lifelink [wb]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either white or black

Whenever this creature hits another creature with an attack, it regains hit points equal to 1d6 + PB.

Double Strike [wr]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either white or red

When this creature successfully lands an attack, it can make another attack against the same target as a bonus action.

Vigilance [wg]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either white or green

When a creature within 5 feet makes an attack against a target other than this creature (and that target doesn’t have vigilance or sentinel), it can use its reaction to make an opportunity attack against that creature.

Additionally, this creature being attacked does not provoke opportunity attacks from its allies using the sentinel feat.

Flash [ub]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either blue or black

This creature can be summoned as a bonus action rather than an action and can be summoned up to 60 ft away.

Prowess [ur]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either blue or red

Whenever you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, this creature has a bonus to attack rolls and saving throws equal to the amount of mana spent until the end of your next turn.

Ward [ug]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either blue or green

This creature has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects and spell attacks made against it are made at disadvantage.

Menace [rb]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either black or red

When a creature starts their turn within 5 feet of this creature or moves within 5 feet of this creature for the first time in a turn, they must make a wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or be frightened of this creature. At the end of their turn, they can repeat the save, ending the effect on a success. When a creature succeeds on a saving throw against this effect, they are immune to this effect from this creature while summoned. When you summon this creature, you may designate any number of creatures to be immune to this ability.

This creature has advantage when attacking frightened targets.

Deathtouch [bg]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either black or green

When this creature hits with an attack, it can choose to do an extra 2d8 poison or necrotic damage (your choice). If this creature is an intermediate creature, increase the damage to 3d10. If this creature is a greater creature, increase the damage to 4d12. It can use this feature once while summoned.

Trample [rg]

Prerequisite: Requires at least 1 devotion to either red or green

When this creature hits with an attack, it can force the target to make a strength saving throw against your spell save DC or be either pushed back 5 feet or be knocked prone. The saving throw is made at disadvantage if the target is 1 or more sizes smaller than this creature.

Additionally, when this creature rolls damage vs an object or structure, it always rolls the maximum amount.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Summon Intermediate Creature

At 6th level, your power heightens as you’re able to summon stronger and more versatile creatures from the multiverse that you’ve encountered or heard tales of in your long journeys. As an action, you summon a creature to an unoccupied point within 30 ft. It goes immediately after your turn in initiative. The creature disappears once it is reduced to 0 hit points, dies, or after 10 minutes. You can also use an action to dismiss it. It is allied to you and follows your verbal commands (no action required by you). If you don’t issue any, it takes the Dodge action and uses its move to avoid danger.

When you summon the intermediate creature, you choose its appearance as well as its creature type from the following list: Beast, Construct, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, Giant, Humanoid, (also choose the race), Monstrosity, Ooze, Plant, or Undead. In addition, you may choose if it has a walking speed or a swimming speed. It uses the following stat block, which uses your proficiency bonus (PB) and planeswalker level (LV) in several places.

When you use this feature, you may select up to 2 different abilities with the modified creatures feature (following normal restrictions). Alternatively, you can give up 2 abilities to instead take any 1 ability from the modified creatures feature, ignoring devotion restrictions.

You can use this feature once and gain the ability to do so again when you finish a short or long rest.

Mana Pool

At 7th level, you’re able to directly tap into the land around you for mana rather than being limited to your own personal well of magic. When you complete a long rest, you can choose to tap into the environment you rested in. When you do so, you gain mana of 1 color equal to your proficiency bonus for that day. You can also prepare spells of that color for that day. The color is determined by the type of land you rested in and is decided by your DM. You know the color before you prepare your spells for the day.

Plains generate white mana, islands generate blue mana, swamps generate black mana, mountains generate red mana, and forests generate green mana. Some environments could be touched by 2 or more colors of mana, in which case your DM will let you choose from a list, such as a misty rainforest being able to generate blue or green mana, or an arid mesa could generate white or red. If a location is artificial, such as a city block or a temple, then the mana is influenced by the types of people that live there and their philosophies. A thieve’s guild likely generates blue or black mana while a temple to a nature spirit might generate green mana.

Planeswalking

When you reach 10th level, you’re able to traverse the multiverse directly. You can cast plane shift without spending mana or requiring material components. You choose the plane. You can only use this spell to transport yourself and other willing creatures, not to banish other creatures.

You can use this feature once and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. At 18th level, you can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Playing In Multiverses

If you’re playing in a setting or campaign that involves jumping between different worlds entirely, such as planescape or the Magic The Gathering Multiverse, your DM may allow you to use this feature to cast Dream of The Blue Veil without expending mana or requiring material components.

In addition, you may choose if it has a walking speed or a swimming speed. It uses the following stat block, which uses your proficiency bonus (PB) and planeswalker level (LV) in several places.

Color Identities

Planeswalkers tap into the 5 colors of mana. All start with 1, but some choose to expand out and connect with many more, while others choose to fully immerse themselves in their first.

White

White mages seek peace through order. White is the color of the collective, whether it be a battalion of well organized soldiers or a town held together by a shared faith. White magic binds communities together and has no tolerance for selfishness or chaos.

White mana is most often represented through healing magic, supportive spells, light, elemental stone, and elemental wind, and its greatest form of creature is the radiant angel.

Life’s Blessing

At 1 devotion to white, when you heal someone, you’re able to infuse additional magic into them to increase their strength. Whenever you or one of your summons restore hit points to yourself or another creature with a spell or ability, they become blessed until the end of your next turn. When they make an attack roll or saving throw while blessed like this, they roll an additional d4 and add the result to the roll. This ability can stack.

Summon Legion

At 2 devotion to white, when you use summon lesser creature, you may spend [w] an amount of times up to your devotion to white. When you do, you summon 1 additional lesser creature with no abilities from modified creature and half the maximum hit points to another point within range for each additional [w] spent. Using this feature does not consume extra uses of Summon Lesser Creature.

Life’s Infusion

At 3 devotion to white, when a creature is blessed due to light’s blessing, the die they roll is increased from 1d4 to 2d4.

Commanding Hierarchy

At 4 devotion to white, you’re able to summon not only creatures but generals for your legion. Your greater summons gain the ability to summon lesser creatures as a bonus action within 30 feet of them. They share your uses of Summon Lesser Creature.

Wrath

At 5 devotion to white, in a great show of force you are able to wipe the battlefield clean of interlopers. As an action, you release a bright flash of light. All lesser creatures and creatures within 60 feet of you who are CR 3 or lower must make a charisma saving throw or be destroyed. A creature destroyed like this is turned to ash.

You can use this feature once and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Blue

Blue mages seek perfection through knowledge. Blue is the color of improvement and efficiency. Blue magic is strongest in places of learning and exploration, such as a wizard’s tower or the high seas. Blue magic drives people to be patient and seek the most optimal and structured path forward in a problem.

Blue mana is most often represented through psychic magic, controlling spells, elemental water, and elemental wind, and its greatest form of creature is the towering leviathan.

Bonus Spells

At 1 devotion to blue, you can store more spells in your mental library and search through it faster. When you prepare spells for the day, you can prepare an additional number of spells equal to your devotion to blue.

Flicker

At 2 devotion to blue, you’re able to shift yourself in and out of space in the same way you do with your summons. As a bonus action, you can teleport 60 ft to an unoccupied space you can see. You can only use this feature if you spent blue mana this turn.





Negate

At 3 devotion to blue, your command of antimagic increases. You’re able to cast counterspell at 3rd level without spending any mana. In addition, when you cast counterspell, you add your proficiency bonus to the ability check.

You can use this feature once and regain the ability to do so when you finish a short rest.

Befuddling Magic

At 4 devotion to blue, you’re able to disable your opponents before they get a chance to cause harm. Whenever a creature fails a saving throw against one of your blue spells, you can give them one of the following effects until the end of your next turn. If the spell was enchantment or illusion, then you may instead make it last as long as the spell.

  • The creature is charmed by you and your summons
  • The creature’s speed is reduced to 0
  • The creature can't speak

Time Walk

At 5 devotion to blue, your magic is able to command the fundamental workings of time itself. When you end your turn, you may take an extra turn after this one.

You can use this feature once and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Black

Black mages seek satisfaction through ruthlessness. Black is the color of ambition and self-reliance. Black magic is drawn from places of decay and desire such as a fetid swamp or a thieve’s guild. Black magic holds close the dark truths of the world: It's kill or be killed and it is best not to die.

Black mana is most often represented through death, poison, acid, madness, and darkness, and its greatest form of creature is the conniving demon.

Life Drain

At 1 devotion to black, you’re able to use your black mana to siphon the life out of your opponents. When you deal necrotic damage, you can regain hit points equal to half the damage done.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your devotion to black and regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Sacrifice

At 2 devotion to black, you’re able to convert the essence of life into mana to fuel your spells. As a bonus action do one of the following.

  • Lose any amount of current hit points. You gain temporary [b] for every 10 hit points you lost. This cannot be reduced in any way.
  • Reduce one of your summons within 60 ft to 0 hit points. You gain temporary [b] for every 10 combined temporary hit points and hit points remaining on the summon, rounded up.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus and regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Demonic Tutor

At 3 devotion to black, you can call upon dark sources for forbidden knowledge. You may cast any spell in the planeswalker spell list as if you had it prepared.

You can use this feature once and regain the ability to do so when you finish a short rest.

Reanimation

At 4 devotion to black, you’re able to summon creatures out of the corpses of your foes (or allies). You may spend [1][b] and pick the corpse of a creature that has died in the last minute. You use either Summon Lesser Creature, Summon Intermediate Creature, or Summon Greater Creature depending of the CR of the creature that died. Doing so does not consume one of the uses of the corresponding feature. When you summon a creature like this, it converts the corpse into the summoned creature and any gear and items they were carrying are held by your summon.

  • CR 1 or higher: Summon Lesser Creature
  • CR 5 or higher: Summon Intermediate Creature
  • CR 10 or higher: Summon Greater Creature

Doom Blade

At 5 devotion to black, you’re able to cause sudden death in your foes. When you deal damage with a spell and reduce your target to an amount of hit points equal to or less than twice your planeswalker level, they are instead reduced to 0 hit points.

Red

Red mages seek freedom through action. Red is the color of passion and impulse. Red magic is drawn from places charged with violence and chaos, like a collapsing cliffside or a bloodsoaked battlefield.

Red mana is most often represented through elemental fire, elemental lightning, elemental stone, and battle, and its greatest form of creature is the ferocious dragon.

Bonus Proficiency

At 1 devotion to red, your magic drives you to the front of the bloodshed. You gain proficiency in martial weapons and shields and can use a martial weapon or a shield as a spellcasting focus.

Haste

At 2 devotion to red, your impulsive magic and actions bleed into your summons. When you summon a creature, they gain an additional action on their next turn.






Burning Magic

At 3 devotion to red, you’re able to heighten your explosive damaging spells. When you roll a 1 or a 2 on a damage die with a spell, you may reroll it. You must use the new rolls.

Duelcaster

At 4 devotion to red, you’re able to enhance your spells with explosive mana and copy their effects. When you cast a spell that targets only one creature and doesn’t have a range of self, you can spend a number of [r] equal to half the spells level, rounded up ([r] for cantrips), to target another creature in range with the same spell. This can be done multiple times within the same spell.

Storm

At 5 devotion to red, you’re able to reverberate your magic through all of your summons. When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you may cause any number of your summons and/or yourself to radiate an explosive burst of magic. All other creatures within 10 feet of the bursts must make a dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC or take 1d6 force damage per mana spent on the spell. A creature can be affected by multiple of these bursts at once.

Green

Green mages seek harmony through acceptance. Green is the color of the natural order of the world. Green magic is drawn from ancient places teeming with life, like an overgrown temple or a fungus infested sewer tunnel.

Green mana is most often represented through plants, animals, and elemental stone, and its greatest form of creature is the colossal dinosaur.

Toughness

At 1 devotion to green, the hardiness of your magic is also infused into your body. Your hit point maximum increases by your planeswalker level and increases by an additional 1 whenever you gain a level in this class.

Giant Growth

At 2 devotion to green, your connections with your summons heightens to the point that you can empower their physical form easily. As a bonus action you can cast the enlarge side of the Enlarge/Reduce spell on one of your summons without spending mana.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your devotion to green and regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Cultivation

At 3 devotion to green, you have a far more intimate attachment to the land you walk on than other mages. You can use the mana pool feature when you complete a short rest in addition to a long rest. When you do, any mana remaining from that ability is replaced with new mana gained by this ability.

You can use this feature like this a number of times equal to your devotion to green and regain all expended uses when you take a long rest.

Hardened Shell

At 4 devotion to green, your summons are more adaptable and able to survive in harsher conditions. Your lesser summons gain +1 to AC and saving throws, your intermediate summons gain +2 to AC and saving throws, and your greater summons gain +3 to AC and saving throws.

Colossal Growth

At 5 devotion to green, your summons have evolved to their apex forms. All of your summons have double health and roll twice the amount of dice when rolling damage for attacks.

Magic Items

Other planeswalkers throughout the multiverse have created magic items to better channel mana and cast spells. Some of their discarded or lost items can be found scattered around its many planes, such as those from the following list.

Mox

Wonderous Item, Uncommon (requires attunement by a planeswalker)

This amulet houses a single gemstone charged with mana. The type of gem indicates the color of the mox, which the DM chooses or determines randomly. You may prepare spells for the day as if you had the mox’s color within your identity.

Additionally, as a free action, you may generate 1 temporary mana of the mox’s color. The mox can do this once and regains the ability to do so daily at dawn.

Gemstone Color
Pearl White [w]
Sapphire Blue [u]
Jet Black [b]
Ruby Red [r]
Emerald Green [g]

Medallion

Wonderous Item, Very Rare, (requires attunement by a planeswalker)

This special medallion resembles its related mox except its gem is fist sized and its magic is far stronger.It cannot be drawn on for mana however, instead it is used as a powerful tool to empower the user’s use of that color. The type of gem indicates the color of the medallion, which the DM chooses or determines randomly. When you cast a spell of the medallion’s color, that spell costs [1] less to cast.

Gemstone Color
Pearl White [w]
Sapphire Blue [u]
Jet Black [b]
Ruby Red [r]
Emerald Green [g]

Sol Ring

Ring, Uncommon,(requires attunement by a planeswalker)

This featureless golden ring is charged with blank mana, devoid of color. As a free action, you can tap into this ring to generate temporary [2]. This mana can only be spent on colorless mana costs in spells such as [1] or [2]. The sol ring can do this once and regains the ability to do so daily at dawn.

Black Lotus

Wonderous Item, Rare

This single delicate flower is brimming with unshaped mana. As a bonus action, you can consume the mana from within the black lotus, causing it to crumple into dust and be destroyed. When you do, you gain 3 temporary mana of any color of your choice.

Gilded Lotus

Wonderous Item, Very Rare

This artificial black lotus has been reinforced with gold, bolstering its longevity. As a bonus action, you can consume the mana from within the gilded lotus. When you do, you gain 3 temporary mana of any one color of your choice. The gilded lotus can do this once and regains the ability to do so daily at dawn.

Lotus Petal

Wonderous Item, Uncommon

This single petal of the fleeting black lotus has been contained within a glass vial. As a bonus action, you can break the vial and consume the mana from within the lotus petal, destroying it forever. When you do, you gain 1 temporary mana of any color of your choice.

Aether Vial

Wonderous Item, Legendary (requires attunement by a planeswalker)

This glass vial is able to filter mana out of the multiverse and slowly fill with its concentrated liquid form. It has 8 charges and regains 1d4 charges daily at dawn.

As an action, you may spend 2 charges and use Summon Lesser Creature without expending one of its uses. Additionally, you may instead spend 5 charges to use Summon Intermediate Creature without expending one of its uses. Lastly, you may instead spend 8 mana to use Summon Greater Creature without expending one of its uses.
