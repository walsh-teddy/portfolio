By Teddy Walsh

The Construct

Reality at its base components is dominated by 2 main forces. Not good and evil, but instead law and chaos, Order and anarchy. There are many beings that act as defenders and enforcers of law. They stand as a bulwark against the forces of chaos. These beings are not made of unpredictable flesh prone to disease and other inflictions, but instead inscritiable metal twisted and assembled into incredibly complex machines. Every part of their form has an intended purpose and works perfectly to achieve that. Many mortals make pacts with these beings to join their great plan, becoming a metaphorical and sometimes literal cog in their greater machines. The gears are always turning.

Examples of Construct warlock patrons include The Inevitables and other powerful beings from the plane of mechanus from The Forgot Realms, The Inexorables from Strongholds and Followers, and Mechane or Anderson Robotics from The SCP Foundation Universe. This could also include advanced AIs and/or massive nanobot hive minds in Science Fiction settings. It is also very common for warforged to become warlocks of these beings.

Quirks of The Construct

d6 roll Quirk
1 A clockwork ticking replaces your heartbeat.
2 Parts of your skin are smooth metal sheets.
3 Steam vents, pistons, and other small machines jut out of your body in places.
4 You don’t move your mouth much when speaking, most of the noise and annunciation instead coming from an unseen mechanical apparatus deep in your throat.
5 You have greatly dampened emotions, opting to value logic and reason as far more important concepts. Emotions can get messy and… chaotic.
6 You commonly mistake objects, especially mechanical parts, as sentient creatures and address them as such.

Expanded Spell List

The construct lets you choose from an expanded list of spells when you learn a warlock spell. The following spells are added to the warlock spell list for you.

Spell Level Spells
1st Command, Identify
2nd Calm Emotions, Zone of Truth
3rd Nondetection, Slow
4th Fabricate, Freedom of Movement
5th Animate Objects, Planner Binding

Bonus Cantrip

At 1st level, you learn the mending cantrip. It counts as a warlock cantrip for you, but it does not count against your number of cantrips known.

Decree of Boundary

At 1st level you gain the ability to impose your rules on someone else’s mind. As an action, target creature within 30 ft of you must succeed on a wisdom saving throw or you choose if the target can either not willingly move closer to you or willingly move farther away from you until the start of your next turn.

You can use this ability a number of times equal to your charisma modifier (minimum of once). You regain all expended uses on a long rest.

Like Clockwork

At 6th level, when you would roll an attack roll, saving throw, or ability check, you may use your reaction before rolling to instead roll a 10 before bonuses. If you would roll multiple D20s, such as through advantage or disadvantage, you instead roll 0 and you get a 10. You may still roll other dice to determine a bonus, such as through the bless spell or the bardic inspiration feature.

Once you use this feature, you cannot use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

Lawful Restraint

At 10th level, when you use your Decree of Boundary feature, you may instead force the target to decide a direction at the start of their turn. Until the start of your next turn they cannot willingly move in any direction other than the one they chose and backwards from that.

In addition, you can use your Decree of Boundary feature as a bonus action or an action and regain expenses uses during a short rest.

Predictable Patterns

At 14th level, you can use your Like Clockwork feature to target other creatures within 60 ft. You may also choose to replace the 10 with an 11 or a 9. Features that allow someone to reroll such as legendary resistance or the lucky feat can still be used.
